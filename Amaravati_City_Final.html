The City · Its Vision · Its Future

Amaravati —
Capital Rising

Conceived on the banks of the Krishna River, Amaravati is Andhra Pradesh's planned capital city — a 21st-century metropolis being built from the ground up as a globally competitive, liveable, and sustainable urban centre. Named after an ancient Buddhist settlement of historic renown, the city blends millennia of heritage with a boldly engineered future.

🌊
Krishna Riverfront
Amaravati stretches along a 10-km stretch of the Krishna River in Guntur district — one of Andhra Pradesh's most fertile and historically significant corridors. The river provides both a scenic identity and a natural resource backbone for water supply, flood management systems, and a planned promenade that will rival the world's great urban waterfronts.
🏛️
Buddhist Heritage
The ancient Amaravati Stupa, dating to the 3rd century BCE, stands nearby — one of the greatest surviving monuments of early Buddhist art in India. The city's name draws directly from this heritage, grounding its ultra-modern vision in a 2,300-year-old legacy of learning, philosophy and cosmopolitan trade.
⚡
Energy Vision
Amaravati targets 100% renewable energy for its government buildings and public infrastructure. Rooftop solar mandates, district-level microgrids, and an advanced SCADA-monitored power network are central to the master plan.
🚌
Mobility Blueprint
A multi-modal transit spine — combining Bus Rapid Transit, last-mile electric shuttles, cycle highways and a proposed metro line — forms the mobility backbone of Amaravati. The Transit Optimizer module models real-time bus deployment needs across the city's commercial and market corridors.
♻️
Zero-Waste City
Amaravati's municipal solid waste strategy mandates source-segregation across all residential layouts. The Sort It! game embedded in this platform trains residents and students to correctly identify wet, dry, hazardous and electronic waste.

Milestones

3rd Century BCE
Ancient Amaravati — Stupa & Buddhist University
The Amaravati Stupa is built under Mauryan patronage, establishing the region as a centre of Buddhist scholarship, art and intercontinental trade connecting Rome to Southeast Asia.
2014
Andhra Pradesh Reorganisation — Capital Needed
Following the bifurcation of Andhra Pradesh and the formation of Telangana, the newly formed state requires a new capital. Amaravati is selected on the banks of Krishna River after an extensive site evaluation.
2015
Foundation Stone Laid by Prime Minister
On 22 October 2015, Prime Minister Narendra Modi and Chief Minister Chandrababu Naidu lay the foundation stone at Uddandarayunipalem, marking the official start of capital construction.
2016 – 2019
Land Pooling & Master Plan Finalised
Over 33,000 acres are acquired through a voluntary land-pooling scheme across 29 villages. Singapore's urban planners collaborate on the master plan; Foster + Partners and other global firms contribute capital complex designs.
2019 – 2022
Political Transitions & Infrastructure Consolidation
A change of government leads to a temporary halt and review. Courts subsequently uphold Amaravati's status as the sole capital. Core infrastructure — roads, drainage, utilities — continues to progress.
2024 – Present
Accelerated Development Phase
New state government commits ₹52,000 crore to Amaravati's revival. Government complex construction resumes at pace. Smart City systems move into active implementation.

About This Platform

Amaravati City is an integrated simulation and education platform supporting the city's Smart City Mission. It brings together renewable energy planning, urban mobility optimisation, and civic education into a single offline-capable dashboard — usable by planners, policymakers, students and citizens alike. All modules run entirely in the browser with no server dependency.
